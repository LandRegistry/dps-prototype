HM Land Registry’s Use Land and Property Data Terms of use

By using this service, you accept these terms of use.

About the service

You can use this service to search for land and property data.

Your search will show you a list of land and property data files from HM Land Registry. This data includes the date new data was last added and when the file is updated. This means that, if you search at 1:00pm, we cannot show you data added to the file at 2:00pm. The service cannot tell you if new data is being added while you search.

If you want to check for ‘new’ data, you can search again and download the latest data at any time. New data is added to the service every month.

For some of the land and property data files you’ll need to enter a separate licence.

The data files you’ll get

Your search will show you a list of big data files in different formats. These files show data about all land and property in England and Wales.

If you’re looking for information about one address or a few addresses, you can use other HM Land Registry services to find this information: https://www.gov.uk/government/organisations/land-registry.

Creating an account

You need an account to use this service.

When you create your account, you may be asked for your credit/debit card details. No payment will be taken and there is no charge for downloading free data. HM Land Registry asks for your card details to identify you.

The information you need to create an account varies depending on who you are and how you plan to use the data.

If you’re using the data for personal use

To create an account, you need to give your:

  • title
  • first name
  • last name
  • email address
  • telephone number
  • address
  • credit or debit card details

You’ll be able to sign in to your account immediately after opening an account.

If you’re using the data as part of your work for a UK company or charity

To create an account, you need to give your:

  • title
  • first name
  • last name
  • email address
  • telephone number
  • organisation address
  • registration number

You may have to wait up to two working days before you can sign in to your account. This is because HM Land Registry will check details about the organisation you work for before you complete your registration.

If you’re using the data as part of your work for an overseas company

To create an account, you need to give your:

  • title
  • first name
  • last name
  • email address
  • telephone number
  • organisation details
  • organisation name
  • country of registration
  • credit or debit card details

You’ll be able to sign in to your account immediately after opening an account.

You cannot change your details once you create an account.

If you need to change your name or address details, you can set up a new account using a different email address.

Keeping your account secure

You are responsible for:

  • keeping your username and password secure
  • all activity that occurs on or through your account

If you think your account security has been breached, contact HM Land Registry.

  • telephone 0300 006 0411
  • speak to a Welsh speaker on 0300 006 0422

HM Land Registry is not responsible for any losses if someone without authorisation uses your account.

If you do not use your account for 12 months, it will be deleted. This is a security measure to protect you and your information. If you want to start using the service again, you’ll need to re-register.

Account closure

Your account will remain live to enable you to use the service at any time. You can ask for your account to be closed and we’ll de-activate your account. To close your account, call HM Land Registry on 0300 006 0411 or to speak to a Welsh speaker, call 0300 006 0422. We will not delete personal data where it is required to establish, defend, or exercise legal claims, for example in relation to property or registration fraud. Personal information will be retained for a minimum period of five years.

If you do not set up an account, the only information we hold about is your IP address. If you create an account, we will hold your:

How we protect your information

HM Land Registry follows the Data Protection Legislation and European Parliament Directives. This means that HM Land Registry uses the personal information you provide in accordance with its personal information charter to:

HM Land Registry only shares your personal information with other organisations if we are allowed to by law.
