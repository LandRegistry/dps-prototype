Data quality (where HM Land Registry gets the data)

Where does the information come from?

The information comes from:

  • citizens
  • corporate bodies
  • conveyancers
  • deeds
  • other documents

Inconsistencies in the data

HM Land Registry data comes from different sources. This means that not everything is written the same way. For example, one address may say ’24 The Willows’ and another might be written as ‘Field south of N14’.

You may also see:

  • spelling errors or typos
  • descriptions instead of addresses (land being developed may not have an address)
  • partial postcodes or no postcode at all
  • mistakes in company registration numbers
  • no data for date of registration
  • no data for company registration number
  • 2 different companies with the same registration number
  • incorrect data due to human error

These inconsistencies are usually either the result of human error (mistakes and typos) or HM Land Registry simply does not have the data.

Think you’ve found a mistake in HM Land Registry data? Contact HM Land Registry: example@hmlandregistry.gov.uk

Included

What is included?

This data shows every (completed) record HM Land Registry has about property-owning companies registered outside the UK.

This includes:

  • Commercial organisations (businesses)
  • limited companies (businesses that are usually owned and run by 1 person)
  • public limited companies (a business with shares)
  • unlimited companies (a business without shares)
  • limited liability partnerships (businesses that are usually owned and run by more than 1 person)

Not included

What is not included?

This data does not show:

  • pending applications (we can’t show you a record while it is being written)
  • UK companies with an overseas address
  • land or property owned by private individuals, charities or UK companies

HM Land Registry can only show you the information we are given.
