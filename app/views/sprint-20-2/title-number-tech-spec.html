Overview

This dataset contains HM Land Registry Title Numbers and AddressBase Unique Property Reference Numbers (UPRN). Customers can use this dataset to make relationships between National Polygon Dataset and other central and local Government data that uses AddressBase UPRNs.

Specification scope

Inclusion and exclusions

The Title Number and UPRN Look Up dataset is a subset of HM Land Registry’s Title Numbers, it is also a subset of the UPRNs supplied from AddressBase. Data is only included where HM Land Registry has made an association between a UPRN and Title Number.

HM Land Registry takes customer addresses at the point of registration and relate them to Ordnance Survey AddressBase data where possible. When a customer address does not relate to an AddressBase address, HM Land Registry will create a new address to relate our title to.

As more AddressBase addresses become available, we match HM Land Registry created addresses to new AddressBase addresses and create relationships where possible.

The title number / UPRN relationship will only be available for those titles where we have been able to make a relationship between our titles and AddressBase addresses.

Relationship to other HM Land Registry data products

As this dataset contains the Title Number, the dataset can be linked to the Title Descriptor and National Polygon datasets (NPD). We have separated this data into three datasets to make each dataset smaller and easier to handle and so that data currency and updates can be more simply managed.

Relationships can be made between the Title Number and UPRN Look Up dataset and other information in the title register by linking through the title number, subject to the download or purchase of additional HM Land Registry data.

Data quality

As part of the address searching, ingest and matching process it is possible that:

  • duplicate addresses may be created because either the information supplied with the application papers or the data in the change only update (COU) does not match the addresses in Land Registry Property Gazetteer (LRPG). This could result in two addresses in LRPG, one with a Title Number and no UPRN, the other with a UPRN but no Title Number.
  • an address exists with a UPRN but is not in LRPG and therefore has no Title Number associated with it.

We welcome feedback on any data quality issues.

Data capture information

In the 1990s, HM Land Registry created its first national address database, populated with residential PAF addresses from Royal Mail. Before this we used addressing data sourced from street card indexes and local gazetteers.

In 2002, we moved to our current addressing database - LRPG (Land Registry Property Gazetteer). LRPG utilised AddressPoint data from the Ordnance Survey to provide a base of national addresses and where appropriate linked our customer address and title numbers to the AddressPoint address.

In 2011, HM Land Registry adopted Ordnance Survey MasterMap and moved from Address-Point to AddressLayer2.

In 2014, HM Land Registry moved from AddressLayer 2 to AddressBase as our source of national addresses. LRPG ingests the post office PAF data from AddressBase to maintain consistency with the addressing that we used to take from AddressPoint and AddressLayer 2. By linking AddressBase data to our Title Numbers we have been able to create the Title Number / UPRN dataset.

A number of post office addresses that do not match Local Authority addresses have been excluded from AddressBase, though more and more are being added all the time, these addresses will not have a UPRN and so will not be available in this dataset.

A significant reason for HM Land Registry creating and managing Land Registry Property Gazetteer (LRPG) is to enable us to make a relationship between our Title Number(s) and address(es). HM Land Registry caseworkers make this association as part of the registration process.

Where there is no address found in LRPG, HM Land Registry creates its own. These addresses could be for non- addressable property or addressable property.

Where a Title Number affects many properties, all affecting UPRNs may be associated.

Where no AddressBase address has been ingested in LRPG, no UPRN will be associated, even though one may exist.

Data maintenance

HM Land Registry takes a six weekly change only update (COU) of AddressBase. The data is ingested, data matching processes are managed with business rules and where appropriate, LRPG is updated and Title associations are made.

During the six weeks, the data can change. The data that is published will only show the last updated change made.

Find out more information on UPRN Lifecycle.

Access to the dataset

There is a charge for the dataset and you will be required to sign a licence.

If you intend to reuse the data within your products and services for commercial gain you must indicate this on the Licence and confirm your choice of reuse model. Contact the Data Services Team for further guidance.

Download package

This package can start at any month of the year, you will be granted access to the Full Dataset, the change only updates and the validation files.

All the files in the above package are refreshed and available from the second working day of the stated months.

File structure

The data is provided as a CSV file. The format of each file will conform to the following:

  • the character used to delimit each field is a comma: ‘,’ (ASCII 44)
  • all fields will be enclosed within double-quote’s “ “ (ASCII 34)
  • when a field is NULL, it will be transmitted as no character between double quotes, i.e. ,” “,
  • date fields will be provided in the format DD-MM-YYYY HH:MM:SS For example the date; the fourteenth of December twenty thirteen would be represented as 14-12-2013
  • the file will use UTF-8 and ASCII encoding.
  • steps will be taken to remove carriage return and line feeds from the data.
  • the column heading name will be the first row of the file.

Naming convention

Full file: LR_UPRN_FULL_<3 character Month>

Validation file: LR_UPRN_VALI_<3 character Month>
