Overview

Commercial and Corporate Ownership is a list of freehold or leasehold registered titles in England and Wales, where the registered legal owner of the land is a non-private individual.

Specification scope

Inclusion and exclusions

HM Land Registry registers legal estates and interests in land. Commercial and Corporate Ownership describes registered legal estates relating to land or property where the registered legal owner is a non-private individual.

The data may contain registrations by the following owner types of:

  • corporations aggregate (a separate legal entity formed by more than one individual person. The corporate aggregate has an existence which is separate from the persons comprising it. The term is used to distinguish them from corporations sole which are legal entities consisting of a single person at any one time)
  • county councils
  • other local authorities
  • housing associations
  • industrial and provident societies
  • registered societies
  • limited companies
  • public limited companies
  • unlimited companies
  • limited liability partnerships
  • community interest companies
  • Societas Europaea (where registered at Companies House)
  • UK Companies with an overseas correspondence address

The dataset excludes titles where the registered owners are:

  • private individuals
  • overseas companies
  • charities including Charitable Incorporated Organisations (CIO)

Where a person or body in any of the three excluded types above is the only registered owner, all details (address, name and correspondence address, tenure, title etc.) are excluded from the dataset.

Where a person or body which is one of these excluded types is intermixed with owner types whose details we can supply, their details are excluded from the name, proprietorship and correspondence address columns.

Example: when a property is owned by a limited company and private individuals, we will supply the details of the property address, tenure, title and the name and correspondence address for the limited company, but will remove all reference to the private individuals. The Additional Proprietor indicator serves to highlight the existence of other proprietors in the register.

Email addresses and British Forces Post Office addresses have been removed from the correspondence address records. These are considered private and personal.

Relationship to other HM Land Registry data products

Commercial and Corporate Ownership Data (CCOD) is a subset of the Land Register containing data describing registered titles relating to land or property where the registered legal owner is a non-private individual and is not a charity or an overseas company. The dataset is complimented by our Overseas companies that own property in England and Wales, a data set of approximately 100,000 records, which describes titles registered to an overseas company (a company incorporated outside of the UK). Together they describe nearly all commercial and corporation aggregate titles registered in England and Wales.

Data quality

During more than 150 years of land registration, changes and improvements to working practice may have introduced some inconsistencies in the data, as shown below.

Only completed register entries are included. No pending applications are included in the dataset (that is applications still being processed at HM Land Registry but not yet completed).

The information provided regarding owners may not always reflect the up to date position in the Register, as we do not include information about private individuals, overseas companies and charities.

The source data is from information supplied by citizens, corporate bodies and conveyancers on registration deeds and documents. There can often be inconsistency in the way in which addresses and names are supplied to HM Land Registry.

There may be spelling errors in the ownership names which reflect the way they have been entered in the Register. This could be due to input error or the way in which the names were presented at the time of registration.

Detailed address information is provided wherever possible.

The postcode will be derived from the registered property description if one has not been registered then the postcode will be blank.

The Date Proprietor Added is the date the proprietor was registered.When HM Land Registry registers a proprietor, the date of registration is usually entered in the register. This date is automatically entered by HM Land Registry casework systems but in some circumstances may be overwritten and left blank, for example, where two individual titles are amalgamated. Approximately 2% of titles in this dataset have a blank field. Where a date is shown this might not be the date that the proprietor was first registered. For example, if a limited company owned a property and then transferred it to itself and another entity, the date shown, if any, will be the date of the registration of the transfer to itself and the other entity.

There are typographic errors in the entry of Company Registration Numbers. This could be due to input error or the way in which the numbers were presented at the time of registration.

It is possible for a Company Registration Number to legitimately be associated with more than one company (for example if a company did not apply to HM Land Registry to update the registered title when a change of name occurred). Clerical errors and/or variations in formatting will result in an apparent mismatch / erroneous relationship between company name and company registration number and vice versa.That said there are genuine errors in this dataset in that erroneous associations and incorrect or missing data has been captured. Approximately 2% of LTD or PLC titles registered after 1.1.1997 have no Company Registration Number. The Land Registration Rules 1996 came into force on 1.1.1997. Prior to this date, there was no obligation for the company registration number to be supplied when making an application for registration so it may not be present in the dataset.

There are thematic errors in the dataset. The HM Land Registry casework process ensures that a Proprietorship type is selected from a drop-down menu. Errors do exist where the incorrect Proprietorship type has been selected.

Every effort has been made to exclude overseas companies, Charities and private individuals from this dataset. It is possible that some do exist.When these are brought to HM Land Registry’s attention the attribution is amended and the incorrect data is removed from the dataset.

Data capture information

A non-private individual will become owner of a registered property when land is transferred to it or leased under a registerable lease. The transfer or lease must be completed by registration, or, if the land is unregistered it will trigger first registration of the land.

An application for registration must be made using prescribed forms; this will be form AP1 for registered land or form FR1 for first registration. The form requires the applicant to state whether any UK company is incorporated under the Companies Acts and if so, to provide the Company Registration Number including any prefix. Other HM Land Registry forms, for example, transfer form TR1, also require the applicant to state whether any UK company is incorporated under the Companies Acts and if so, to provide the Company Registration Number including any prefix. When HM Land Registry enter a company in the register, HM Land Registry practice is to enter the company registration number. This has been the case since 1997. HM Land Registry does not validate the information supplied to Companies House. Other types of corporations may be allocated a number by their registration authority.

HM Land Registry takes customer addresses at the point of registration and relates them to AddressBase data where possible. When a customer address does not relate to an AddressBase address, HM Land Registry will create a new address to relate our Title Number to.

Data maintenance

During a month the data can change and may also change more than once.The data that is published will only show the last updated change made.

Data product delivery

Access to the dataset

The Commercial and Corporate Ownership dataset is not released under the Open Government Licence. To download the data, you must provide some information about yourself and accept the terms of use.

We collect and hold personal and non-personal information, which may be validated, to identify who has requested the data. For more information about how HM Land Registry handle personal data, see the personal information charter.

Once approved, access to the dataset will be via an online secure data room. An application followed by the agreement to the relevant terms of use will lead to the creation of an account. Log on information will then be issued.

If you need support to access the data, contact the Data Services Team for further guidance.

File structure

The data is provided as a CSV file. The format of each file will conform to the following:

  • the character used to delimit each field is a comma: , (ASCII 44)
  • all fields will be enclosed within double quotes: “ ” (ASCII 34)
  • when a field is NULL, it will be transmitted as no character between double quotes: ,“ ”,
  • date fields will be provided in the format DD-MM-YYYY. For example, the date ‘the fourteenth of December twenty thirteen’ would be represented as 14-12-2013
  • the file will use UTF-8 and ASCII encoding
  • steps will be taken to remove carriage return and line feeds from the data
  • the column heading name will be the first row of the file

File name

The file names will be structured as below.

CCOD_<1><2><3><4>.csv

<1>File Type. FULL denotes full monthly extract, COU denotes Monthly COU.

><2>Month of file creation (format MM).

<3>Year of file creation (format yyyy).

<4>Due to limited file sizes this relates to which part of the file that is being accessed.
